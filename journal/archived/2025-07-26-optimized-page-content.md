# How We Support Neurodivergent Learners: ADHD and Montessori

*By Sarah Williams, Inclusion Specialist*
*March 15, 2024*

## Why Montessori Works Beautifully for Children with ADHD

If your child has ADHD, you've probably heard well-meaning advice about structure, sitting still, and focusing. But what if we told you that movement, choice, and hands-on learning could be exactly what your child needs?

At Spicebush Montessori School in Glen Mills, we've seen countless children with ADHD not just succeed—but absolutely thrive in our prepared environment. Here's why Montessori might be the perfect fit for your energetic, creative, divergent thinker.

### Movement Is Learning, Not a Distraction

In traditional classrooms, children with ADHD often hear "sit still" and "pay attention" countless times each day. But Dr. Montessori understood something crucial: movement and learning are connected.

In our Glen Mills classrooms, children:
- Choose where to work (table, floor mat, standing)
- Move freely between activities
- Use materials that require physical manipulation
- Take movement breaks whenever needed
- Work in positions that feel comfortable

"When James can move while learning, he actually focuses better," shares his mom, a Chester County resident. "At Spicebush, movement isn't seen as disruptive—it's part of the process."

### Individualized Pace Eliminates Pressure

ADHD brains often work in bursts of intense focus followed by necessary mental breaks. Our three-hour work cycles accommodate this natural rhythm:

- No bells forcing transitions
- Freedom to hyperfocus when engaged
- Permission to pause and return
- Choice in activity duration
- Self-paced progression

This means your child can spend 90 minutes absorbed in building the pink tower or take quick 5-minute work periods throughout the morning—whatever matches their needs that day.

### Concrete Materials Make Abstract Concepts Clear

Many children with ADHD are kinesthetic learners who need to touch, manipulate, and experience concepts. Montessori materials are perfect for this:

**Math Becomes Tangible**
- Golden beads make place value concrete
- Fraction circles show relationships
- Geometry sticks build understanding

**Language Comes Alive**
- Sandpaper letters engage multiple senses
- Moveable alphabet allows experimentation
- Object boxes connect words to reality

**Practical Life Builds Focus**
- Pouring activities develop concentration
- Food preparation provides immediate results
- Care tasks offer purposeful movement

### Structure With Freedom

Children with ADHD thrive with clear expectations and predictable routines—but also need autonomy. Montessori provides both:

**Environmental Structure**
- Everything has a specific place
- Materials organized by difficulty
- Clear visual boundaries
- Consistent daily rhythm

**Personal Freedom**
- Choice of work
- Choice of workspace
- Choice of work partner
- Choice of duration

### Real Strategies We Use Daily

At our Glen Mills campus, we support children with ADHD through:

**Environmental Adaptations**
- Quiet corners for regulation
- Standing work stations
- Wobble cushions and balance boards
- Noise-reducing headphones available
- Natural lighting throughout

**Visual Supports**
- Picture schedules for those who need them
- Work plan cards
- Visual timers (optional)
- Color-coding systems

**Sensory Tools**
- Stress balls and fidgets available
- Heavy work opportunities
- Sensory breaks built in
- Calming corner with tools

**Executive Function Support**
- Breaking large tasks into steps
- Visual organization systems
- Transition warnings when needed
- Celebration of completion
