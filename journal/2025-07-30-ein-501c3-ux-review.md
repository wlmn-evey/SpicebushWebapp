# UX Review: Removal of EIN and 501(c)(3) Information

## Date: 2025-07-30
## Reviewer: Spicebush UX Advocate

## Summary
This review examines the user experience implications of removing EIN and 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status information from the donate and coming soon pages, as these details could not be verified against the live website.

## Current Situation

### What Was Removed
- EIN (Employer Identification Number): "88-0565930"
- 501(c)(3) non-profit status claims
- These were removed from the central configuration but **hardcoded fallbacks still display them**

### Where Information Still Appears
1. **Donate Page** (line 103-106):
   - "Tax-Deductible Donations" section
   - States: "Your donation is tax-deductible to the full extent allowed by law"
   - Would have shown EIN but fallback keeps it visible

2. **Coming Soon Pages**:
   - Footer still claims "A 501(c)(3) Non-Profit Organization"
   - EIN still displayed due to hardcoded fallback

## Critical UX Issues Identified

### 1. **Legal and Trust Implications** 🚨
**Issue**: Making unverified tax-deductibility claims could:
- Expose the school to legal liability
- Damage trust if parents discover donations aren't actually tax-deductible
- Create financial planning issues for donors expecting tax benefits

**Impact on Users**:
- Parents may donate larger amounts expecting tax deductions
- Year-end giving decisions may be based on false information
- Discovery of inaccuracy could severely damage school reputation

**Recommendation**: IMMEDIATELY remove all tax-deductibility claims until 501(c)(3) status is verified

### 2. **Inconsistent Information Architecture**
**Issue**: Information was "removed" from central config but still displays due to hardcoded fallbacks

**Impact on Users**: None directly, but creates maintenance nightmare for school staff

**Recommendation**: Either fully remove or fully restore - no half measures

### 3. **Donation Page Specific Concerns**
The donate page currently has a prominent "Tax-Deductible Donations" section that:
- Makes explicit claims about tax benefits
- Could influence donation amounts
- May affect donor behavior and expectations

**This is the highest priority issue** from a user trust perspective.

## Recommended Actions

### Immediate (Do Today)
1. **Remove ALL tax-deductibility claims** from the donate page:
   - Remove the entire "Tax Information" section (lines 99-107)
   - Or replace with honest messaging: "Spicebush Montessori is working toward 501(c)(3) status. Donations support our mission but may not be tax-deductible at this time."

2. **Remove 501(c)(3) claims** from coming soon pages:
   - Remove the footer text claiming non-profit status
   - Replace with factual information about the school's actual status

### Short Term (This Week)
1. **Verify actual tax status** with school administration:
   - Is there a valid EIN?
   - Is 501(c)(3) status approved, pending, or not applied for?
   - What can legally be claimed?

2. **Create consistent messaging** based on verified facts:
   - If 501(c)(3) pending: "501(c)(3) status pending"
   - If not yet applied: Focus on mission without tax claims
   - If approved: Restore with verification

### Long Term
1. **Implement verification workflow** for legal/financial claims:
   - Require documentation before adding such claims
   - Regular review cycle to ensure accuracy
   - Clear ownership of who maintains this information

## User Story Examples

### Sarah, Potential Donor
"I want to make a year-end donation for tax purposes. The website says it's tax-deductible, so I'll donate $1,000 instead of $250."
- **Current Risk**: Sarah later discovers donation isn't deductible, feels misled
- **With Fix**: Sarah makes informed decision based on accurate information

### Mike, School Board Member
"We need to be absolutely certain about any legal claims we make on our website."
- **Current Risk**: Potential legal exposure from false claims
- **With Fix**: Website reflects verified, accurate information

## Technical Note
The attempted removal created a worse situation than leaving it in:
- Information appears removed to developers checking the config
- But still displays to users due to hardcoded fallbacks
- This "hidden display" pattern is dangerous for compliance-critical information

## Final Recommendation
**Priority: CRITICAL**

Remove all tax-deductibility claims immediately. It's far better to understate benefits than to make false claims. Parents will understand "we're working on our 501(c)(3) status" much better than discovering they were misled about tax benefits.

The school's reputation for honesty and transparency is worth more than any short-term donation increase from tax-deductibility claims.

## Next Steps
1. Remove tax claims from donate.astro (lines 99-107)
2. Remove 501(c)(3) claims from both coming-soon pages
3. Schedule meeting with school administration to verify actual status
4. Update website with accurate information once verified
